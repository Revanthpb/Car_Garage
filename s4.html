Lamborghini Aventador SVJ Guide

Overview

The Lamborghini Aventador SVJ is the ultimate expression of Lamborghini's supercar DNA. With its naturally aspirated V12 engine, advanced aerodynamics, and cutting-edge technology, the SVJ (Super Veloce Jota) is designed to dominate both the road and the track. It's a car that combines raw power with precision engineering.

Key Features

  • Engine: 6.5-liter naturally aspirated V12 producing 759 horsepower and 531 lb-ft of torque.
  • Top Speed: 217 mph (350 km/h).
  • Acceleration: 0-60 mph in just 2.8 seconds.
  • Aerodynamics: Features Lamborghini's ALA (Aerodinamica Lamborghini Attiva) system for maximum downforce and stability.
  • Exclusivity: Limited production run, making it one of the rarest and most desirable Lamborghinis ever made.

Performance

  • Power Output: 759 horsepower and 531 lb-ft of torque.
  • Transmission: 7-speed ISR (Independent Shifting Rod) automated manual transmission.
  • Braking: Carbon-ceramic brakes for exceptional stopping power.
  • Weight: 3,362 lbs (1,525 kg) with a focus on lightweight construction.
  • Handling: All-wheel drive with rear-wheel steering for unparalleled agility.

Technology

  • ALA System: Active aerodynamics that adapts to driving conditions for optimal performance.
  • Driving Modes: Multiple driving modes, including Strada (Street), Sport, and Corsa (Track).
  • Lightweight Construction: Extensive use of carbon fiber and lightweight materials.
  • Infotainment: Advanced infotainment system with a digital cockpit and connectivity features.
