Ford GT Guide

Overview

The Ford GT is a legendary supercar that pays homage to the iconic GT40, which dominated Le Mans in the 1960s. With its sleek design, cutting-edge technology, and blistering performance, the Ford GT is a modern marvel of automotive engineering. It combines race-bred performance with everyday drivability, making it a true masterpiece.

Key Features

  • Engine: 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 producing 660 horsepower and 550 lb-ft of torque.
  • Top Speed: Over 216 mph (348 km/h).
  • Acceleration: 0-60 mph in just 2.8 seconds.
  • Weight: 3,054 lbs (1,385 kg) thanks to extensive use of carbon fiber and lightweight materials.
  • Exclusivity: Limited production run, making it one of the most sought-after supercars in the world.

Performance

  • Power Output: 660 horsepower and 550 lb-ft of torque.
  • Transmission: 7-speed dual-clutch automatic gearbox.
  • Braking: Carbon-ceramic brakes for exceptional stopping power.
  • Handling: Advanced aerodynamics and active suspension for maximum grip and stability.
  • Track Capability: Designed to deliver exceptional performance on both the road and the track.

Technology

  • Aerodynamics: Active aerodynamics with a rear wing and front splitter for optimal downforce.
  • Lightweight Construction: Extensive use of carbon fiber and titanium for reduced weight.
  • Interior: Driver-focused cockpit with premium materials and advanced technology.
  • Exhaust System: Titanium exhaust system for a distinctive and powerful sound.
